About Me

Who I Am

I am an atmospheric scientist with a deep interest in the physical processes that govern extreme weather. My career has taken me across three continents — from Bangladesh, where I grew up, to Germany, where I trained in environmental physics and remote sensing, to the United States, where I earned my Ph.D. and now work at NCAR.

My doctoral work at the University of Arizona focused on improving convective-permitting model simulations of extreme precipitation through object-based tracking and data assimilation. As a postdoc at NCAR's Research Applications Laboratory, I continue this work with an emphasis on operational forecasting and machine learning.

Beyond science, I'm a poet and a cricket all-rounder — I believe curiosity, whether on the field or in the atmosphere, is the best compass.

Research

What I Work On

My work sits at the intersection of numerical modeling, observations, and data-driven methods — focused on understanding and predicting high-impact weather events.

🌩️

Numerical Weather Forecasting

Convective-permitting simulations using WRF and CESM over complex terrain to improve precipitation forecasts.

📡

Data Assimilation

Integrating GPS precipitable water vapor and multi-sensor observations into numerical models to improve forecast accuracy.

🛰️

MCS Tracking

Object-based tracking of mesoscale convective systems linked to extreme precipitation events across North America.

🤖

Machine Learning

Applying ML techniques to weather forecasting, storm classification, and climate variability analysis.

🌧️

North American Monsoon

Studying convective dynamics and precipitation predictability using observing system simulation experiments (OSSEs).

🔭

Remote Sensing

Satellite image analysis, DOAS, FTIR spectroscopy, and cloud radiative forcing from GOME-2 measurements.

DART Equation Explanations

An equation-driven walkthrough of the Data Assimilation Research Testbed — covering the EAKF state update, forward operator, localization, inflation, and a full variable glossary.

01Overview: mean state sequential update
Core update equation
Analysis from forecast
\[\bar{x}_a = \bar{x}_f + (\alpha \cdot \beta) \left(\frac{\lambda'\sigma_f^2}{\sigma_o^2 + \lambda'\sigma_f^2}\right)\!\left(y_o - \overline{h(x)}\right)\]
\(\alpha\) — localization \(\beta\) — sensitivity \(\lambda'\) — inflation \(y_o\) — GPS-PWV
Ensemble state vector \(x_\text{ens}\)
T, U, V, W, Q, PH, MU
T2, Q2, U10, V10, TH2, PS, PBLH
Qrain, Qcloud, Qgraupel, Qsnow, Qice
QNrain, QNice
All variables can be updated even though only PWV is directly observed — via ensemble covariance.
Forward operator h(x)
\[h(x) = \frac{1}{\rho_{h_2o} g}\sum_{k=1}^{n_k-1}\frac{1}{2}|Q_k-Q_{k+1}|\cdot|P_k-P_{k+1}|\]
\(Q_k, P_k\) extracted from \(\mathcal{X}\) and bilinearly interpolated
Inflation \(\lambda\)
\[\lambda' = (1-\!\sqrt{\lambda})^2,\quad \lambda = \frac{(y_o - \bar{y}_f)^2}{\sigma_o^2 + \sigma_f^2}\]
Ensemble-based sensitivity
\[\frac{\Delta x}{\Delta y} = \frac{\operatorname{cov}(x_f,y_f)}{\operatorname{var}(y_f)}\]
02EAKF state update details
Given an \(n_j\)-member ensemble of forecast state \(x_f\) and scalar observation \(y_o\) with error variance \(\sigma_o^2\), both the mean \(\bar{x}_f\) and all members \(j\) are updated as \(\bar{x}_a\) and \(x_a^j\).
Ensemble statistics
\[\bar{x}_f = \frac{1}{n_j}\!\sum_j x_f^j,\quad \sigma_f^2 = \frac{1}{n_j}\!\sum\!\left(x_f^j - \bar{x}_f\right)^2\]
Posterior mean expected obs
\[\bar{y}_a = \sigma_a^2\!\left[\sigma_f^{\prime -2}\cdot\bar{y}_f + \sigma_o^{-2}\cdot\bar{y}_o\right]\]
Posterior variance
\[\sigma_a^2 = \left[\sigma_f^{\prime -2} + \sigma_o^{-2}\right]^{-1}\]
Analysis increment
\[\Delta y_a^j = \!\left(\sqrt{\sigma_o^2(\sigma_o^2+\sigma_f^2)^{-1}}\right)\!\left(y_f^j - \bar{y}_f\right)+\left(\bar{y}_a - \bar{y}_f^j\right)\]
\[\Delta x_a^j = x_a^j - x_f^j = (\alpha\cdot\beta)\cdot\Delta y_a^j\]
Member update
\[x_a^j = x_f^j + (\alpha\cdot\beta)\cdot\Delta y_a^j\]
Full mean update
\[\bar{x}_a = \bar{x}_f + (\alpha\cdot\beta)\!\left(\frac{\sigma_f^2}{\sigma_o^2+\sigma_f^2}\right)\!\left(y_o - \overline{h(x_f)}\right)\]
Inflation factor
\[\lambda'=(1-\!\sqrt{\lambda})^2\]
\[\lambda=\frac{(y_o-\bar{y}_f)^2}{\sigma_o^2+\sigma_f^2},\quad\sigma_f^{\prime 2}=\lambda'\!\cdot\!\sigma_f^2\]
03State vector and mean update formula
State vector variable groups
Dynamical & thermodynamical
T, U, V, W, Q, PH, MU
temperature, winds, water vapor, geopotential, dry air mass
Near-surface / boundary layer
T2, Q2, U10, V10, TH2, PS, PBLH
2 m temp & humidity, 10 m winds, surface pressure, BL height
Hydrometeors
Qrain, Qcloud, Qgraupel, Qsnow, Qice
Number concentrations
QNrain, QNice
1. Mean state sequential update
\[\bar{x}_a = \bar{x}_f + \Delta x\]
\(\bar{x}_f\)ensemble-mean forecast (prior)
\(\bar{x}_a\)ensemble-mean analysis (posterior)
\(\Delta x\)analysis increment — how much state is corrected
3. Full mean update formula
\[\bar{x}_a = \bar{x}_f + (\alpha\cdot\beta)\!\left(\frac{\lambda'\sigma_f^2}{\sigma_o^2+\lambda'\sigma_f^2}\right)\!\left(y_o - h(x)\right)\]
4. Innovation: \((y_o - h(x))\)
\(y_o\)actual GPS-PWV observation
\(h(x)\)expected observation from the model
\(y_f = h(x_f)\)ensemble-mean expected observation
Positive → model too dry · Negative → model too moist. This is the error signal that drives the update.
04Forward operator, weighted innovation & localization
5. Forward operator h(x)
\[h(x)=\frac{1}{\rho_{h_2o}g}\sum_{k=1}^{n_k-1}\frac{1}{2}(Q_k+Q_{k+1})(P_k-P_{k+1})\]
\(Q_k\)humidity at level \(k\)
\(P_k\)pressure at level \(k\)
Vertically integrates specific humidity → PWV. Values bilinearly interpolated to observation location.
6. Weighted innovation term
\[\frac{\lambda'\sigma_f^2}{\sigma_o^2+\lambda'\sigma_f^2}\]
\(\sigma_f^2\)prior ensemble variance
\(\sigma_o^2\)observation error variance
\(\lambda'\)inflation-adjusted factor
\(\sigma_f^2 \gg \sigma_o^2\) → large update · \(\sigma_o^2 \gg \sigma_f^2\) → small update. The Kalman weighting.
7. Ensemble-based sensitivity \(\beta\)
\[\beta = \frac{\operatorname{cov}(x_f,y_f)}{\operatorname{var}(y_f)}\]
Large \(\beta\) → variable strongly correlated with PWV. Small \(\beta\) → weak update. This is why PWV can update winds, temperature, PBLH, etc.
8. Localization function \(\alpha\)
Distance
\[z=\sqrt{z_\text{horiz}^2+z_\text{vert}^2}\]
Horizontal + vertical separation in radians
Gaspari–Cohn function
\[\alpha(z,c)=\begin{cases}1 & |z|=0\\\text{smooth decay} & 0<|z|<2c\\0 & |z|\geq 2c\end{cases}\]
Prevents spurious long-range correlations. Limits PWV influence to physically nearby grid points.
05Inflation, prior inflation & physical interpretation
9. Inflation \((\lambda,\lambda')\)
\[\lambda=\frac{(y_o-y_f)^2}{\sigma_o^2+\sigma_f^2},\quad\lambda'=(1-\!\sqrt{\lambda})^2\]
Detects under-dispersed ensemblesincreases spread where innovation is large
Spatially-varying Gaussian inflationwithout inflation → ensemble collapse → filter failure
10. Prior inflation
\[\lambda'\sigma_f^2\]
Forecast variance inflated before update. Ensures observations continue to have impact.
11. Observation type: GPS-PWV
Column-integrated moisture observation. Strongly constrains lower-mid tropospheric humidity, boundary-layer structure, and moisture convergence. Through covariance, PWV indirectly updates temperature, winds, pressure, and hydrometeors.
12. Full physical interpretation
  1. 1Model predicts PWV via \(h(x)\)
  2. 2Compare with observed PWV → innovation
  3. 3Weight innovation using \(\sigma_o^2, \sigma_f^2\)
  4. 4Spread update using covariance \(\beta\)
  5. 5Limit influence using localization \(\alpha\)
  6. 6Maintain spread using inflation
  7. 7Update all correlated state variables
06Glossary: ensemble states & observations
Basic ensemble and states
\(x^\text{ens}\)
Ensemble
Collection of model state realizations representing atmospheric uncertainty.
\(x_f\)
Forecast state (prior)
Model state before assimilating the new observation.
\(x_a\)
Analysis state (posterior)
Updated model state after assimilation.
\(\bar{x}_f,\;\bar{x}_a\)
Ensemble mean
Average of all members. \(\bar{x}_f = \frac{1}{n_j}\sum_j x_f^j\)
\(\sigma_f^2\)
Ensemble covariance
Variance of ensemble around its mean — measures spread and uncertainty.
Observations and innovations
\(y_o\)
Observation
Measured GPS-PWV with known error variance \(\sigma_o^2\).
\(y_f = h(x_f)\)
Expected observation
Model's estimate via forward operator applied to the forecast state.
\(\Delta y = y_o - y_f\)
Innovation
Difference between actual and model-expected observation — what the filter corrects.
weighted \(\Delta y\)
Weighted innovation
Innovation scaled by error variances and localization/inflation factors.
07Glossary: increments, covariances & forward operator
Analysis increments and member updates
\(\Delta x = x_a - x_f\)
State increment
Change applied to the state by assimilation.
\(\Delta y_a\)
Analysis increment in obs space
Change in each member's expected observation during update.
\(x_a^j\)
Member update
\[x_a^j = x_f^j + (\alpha\beta)\,\Delta y_a^j\]
Updated state for ensemble member \(j\).
Covariances and sensitivities
\(\operatorname{Cov}(x_f,y_f)\)
State-obs covariance
Links model space and observation space across the ensemble.
\(\operatorname{Var}(y_f)\)
Expected-obs variance
Part of the Kalman gain denominator.
\(\beta = \operatorname{Cov}/\operatorname{Var}\)
Ensemble-based sensitivity
How much a state variable should respond to the observed innovation.
Forward operator and variables
\(h(x)\)
Forward operator
Maps model state → observation space. Converts 3D water-vapor fields and pressure to PWV via vertical integral.
Obs ~ PWV
GPS-PWV observation
Total column precipitable water — the quantity being assimilated.
T, U, V, W, Q, PH, MU, …
Model state variables
Temperature, wind components, water vapor mixing ratio, geopotential, and others composing \(x^\text{ens}\).
08Glossary: localization, inflation & EAKF
Localization
\(z\)
Localization distance
\[z=\sqrt{|horiz|^2+|vert|^2}\]
Horizontal + vertical separation between observation and state (radians).
\(\alpha(z,c)\)
Localization function
Gaspari–Cohn taper that reduces observation influence with distance, going to zero beyond \(2c\).
\(c\)
Radius parameter
Cutoff controlling how far observations influence the state.
Inflation
\(\lambda\)
Inflation factor
\[\lambda=\frac{(y_o-y_f)^2}{\sigma_o^2+\sigma_f^2}\]
Inflates prior variance to counteract ensemble under-dispersion.
\(\lambda'\)
Modified inflation
\[\lambda'=(1-\sqrt{\lambda})^2\]
Scales \(\sigma_f^2\) to \(\lambda'\sigma_f^2\) for smoother, more stable inflation.
prior inflation
Application of \(\lambda'\)
Applied to the forecast ensemble before assimilation. Often spatially varying and Gaussian.
EAKF-specific details
EAKF
Ensemble Adjustment Kalman Filter
Deterministic ensemble filter that adjusts members so the updated ensemble exactly matches the Kalman mean and variance. No perturbed observations needed.
\(\bar{y}_a\)
Posterior mean expected obs
Mean expected observation after assimilation, using posterior variance combining \(y_f\) and \(y_o\) with precision weights.
\(\sigma_a^2\)
Posterior variance
\[\sigma_a^2=\left[\sigma_f^{\prime -2}+\sigma_o^{-2}\right]^{-1}\]
Updated variance in observation space — always smaller than both prior and observation variances.
The EAKF is the filter implemented in DART for deterministic ensemble assimilation. These equations explain how DART updates the WRF ensemble given GPS-PWV observations.
