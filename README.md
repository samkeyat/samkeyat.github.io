## About Me

I'm an atmospheric scientist passionate about understanding the physical processes that drive weather and climate. My work sits at the intersection of numerical modeling, data assimilation, and machine learning — with a focus on improving our ability to forecast extreme precipitation events.

I'm an atmospheric scientist passionate about weather and climate. Currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at NSF-NCAR, I specialize in numerical weather forecasting, data assimilation, and tracking mesoscale convective systems, also using machine learning.

## Research

My research focuses on:

- **Convective-Permitting Modeling** — Simulating extreme precipitation at high resolution using the WRF model
- **Data Assimilation** — Integrating multi-sensor observations (e.g., GPS precipitable water vapor) into numerical models to improve forecast accuracy
- **Mesoscale Convective System (MCS) Tracking** — Linking storm tracks to extreme precipitation using satellite-driven and ensemble modeling approaches
- **Machine Learning** — Applying ML techniques to weather forecasting and climate analysis
- **North American Monsoon** — Studying convective dynamics and precipitation predictability over complex terrain

**Field Experience:**  
Participated in the **EUREC4A Field Campaign** (Barbados, Jan–Feb 2020), operating the VELOX infrared imager and SMART spectrometer aboard the DLR-HALO research aircraft.
